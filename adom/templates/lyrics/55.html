1. My God, how wonderful Thou art, Thy majesty how bright! How beautiful Thy mercy-seat, In depths of burning light!

2 How dread are Thine eternal years, O everlasting Lord, By holy angels, day and night, Incessantly adored!

3 O how I fear Thee, living God, With deepest, tenderest fears; And worship Thee with trembling hope, And penitential tears.

4 Yet I may love Thee too, O Lord, Almighty as Thou art; For Thou hast stooped to ask of me The love of my poor heart.

5 No earthly father loves like Thee, No mother e'er so mild Bears and forbears, as Thou hast done With me, Thy sinful child.

6 How wonderful, how beautiful, The sight of Thee must be, Thine endless wisdom, boundless power, And aweful purity!
