1. When I survey the wondrous Cross On which the Prince of glory died My richest gain I count but loss And pour contempt on all my pride

2 Forbid it Lord that I should boast Save in the death of Christ my God All the vain things that charm me most I sacrifice them to His Blood

3 See from His head His hands His feet Sorrow and love flow mingled down Did eer such love and sorrow meet Or thorns compose so rich a crown

4 His dying crimson like a robe Spreads oer His body on the tree Then I am dead to all the globe And all the globe is dead to me

5 Were the whole realm of nature mine That were an offering far too small Love so amazing so divine Demands my soul my life my all
